The Wolf School is one of the five major witcher schools in the Witcher universe. It was founded by the legendary witcher Kitsu, who established the school's philosophy of balance between combat prowess and alchemical knowledge. The school is located at Kaer Morhen, a fortress in the mountains that serves as both training ground and home for its witchers.

The Wolf School's symbol is a stylized wolf's head, representing the pack mentality and loyalty that the school emphasizes. Unlike other schools that may focus on specific aspects like brute strength (Bear School) or stealth (Cat School), the Wolf School takes a balanced approach to witcher training.

Combat training at the Wolf School emphasizes speed, agility, and precision. Students learn to use both steel swords for humans and silver swords for monsters. The school's signature fighting style involves quick, successive strikes combined with strategic dodging and parrying. Wolf School witchers are particularly adept at using signs in combat, especially Quen for defense and Aard for crowd control.

Geralt of Rivia is the most famous graduate of the Wolf School. His training there gave him exceptional reflexes, mastery of multiple signs, and proficiency with various weapons. The school's emphasis on alchemy also made Geralt skilled in potion crafting, bomb making, and oil preparation for different monster types.

Kaer Morhen serves multiple purposes for the Wolf School. It is a fortress that protects witchers from persecution, a training ground where new witchers undergo their mutations and combat training, and a repository of ancient knowledge about monsters and alchemy. The fortress contains laboratories, training arenas, and living quarters for the witchers.

The school's philosophy extends beyond combat to include a code of ethics. Wolf School witchers are taught to take contracts only on genuine monsters, not on humans or political targets. This code has been maintained throughout the school's history, though individual witchers may interpret it differently.

In the modern era of the Witcher timeline, the Wolf School has declined in numbers. Geralt and Vesemir are among the last remaining witchers from this school. However, their skills and knowledge continue to influence new generations through Geralt's adopted daughter Ciri, who possesses even greater abilities than traditional witchers.
