Shar is the goddess of darkness, loss, and secrets in the Forgotten Realms. She is one of the most ancient deities and represents the void between stars. In Baldur's Gate 3, Shar serves as the main antagonist who seeks to plunge the world into eternal darkness. Her motivations stem from her eternal conflict with her twin sister Selune, the goddess of light and moon.

Selune is the goddess of the moon, stars, navigation, and hope. She represents everything that Shar opposes: light, clarity, and revelation. The two sisters have been locked in an eternal struggle since the dawn of time. This conflict is not merely philosophical but has tangible effects on the world of Faerun.

The Netherstones are powerful artifacts created by Shar to extend her influence. These stones are fragments of her divine power and can control the tadpole infection that affects the player character. The Netherstones also maintain the Shadow-Cursed Lands, areas of Faerun permanently shrouded in darkness where normal vision is impossible.

In the main storyline, players encounter the conflict between Shar and Selune through various quests and companions. The Moonrise Towers serve as Shar's stronghold in the region, while the Grove represents Selune's influence. Players must eventually choose which deity to support, and this decision has significant consequences for the game's ending.

The conflict affects gameplay mechanics as well. Areas under Shar's influence are shrouded in darkness, requiring darkvision or magical light sources. Companions have their own allegiances - some support Selune's light while others are drawn to Shar's power. The player's choices throughout the game determine which ending they receive, ranging from embracing Shar's darkness to defeating her and restoring Selune's light.
